After School Programs

Education is an attitude and an approach and should extend outside the limits and scope of the classroom. After school programs allow for expanded and extended interests and development of skills and abilities. Center Park School offers several options, organized and directed by dedicated, passionate, volunteer faculty members. Our programs are scheduled to be compatible with one another. No program requires hours outside of school for continued participation, however practice makes perfect, and suggested assignments, exercises, or hours may be sent home with each student. Do regular homework first!


How much is too much?

While after school programs at Center Park School are designed and scheduled to be compatible, so that students do not have to choose only one, there is a firm rule than no student can sign up or participate in more than 3 programs! These programs are meant to be an addition to the standard education experience and should not interfere with regular homework and study. This rule also helps us ensure we can work with all students despite limited resources- without additional costs and fees.

  • Intramural Athletics and Sports

    • M-F 5-6pm

    Coaches will instruct students in 30 minutes of drills for a chosen sport each day, then officiate a 30 minute game. Sports vary day to day, depending on season, and weather.

    This club is free to join.

  • Music Band

    • M-F 3-4pm

    Students will learn a variety of instruments, depending on availability, or can learn one single instrument, if the student brings their own instrument each day. Various musical styles will be practiced, including marches, and classical.

    This club costs $10 per semester, if the student will play pool instruments kept by the school.

  • Present and Future Artists

    • T,R 4-5pm

    Students will gain instruction in various artistic styles and methods, including watercolor painting, sketching, paper mache, and collage.

    Fees are $20 per semester.

  • Quiz Bowl Team

    • M 4-5pm

    Students will answer trivia in teams on subjects including history, geography, math, science, and literature.

    This club is free to join.

  • Debate Team

    • W 4-5pm

    Our award-winning debate team will teach the student participant how to argue and discuss effectively. The student will never whine again, but will rather use logic and reason to expostulate his or her thoughts and feelings. Armed with the dialectic, the student will be no one's fool and will be armed for future interviews and application processes, ranging far beyond just public speaking!

    Fees for this club

  • Math Club

    • F 4-5pm

    Students will race against time, each other, and their own limitations solving addition, subtraction, long division, multiplication, word problems, and number-related riddles and visual puzzles.

    This club is free to join
