Stylized CSS Buttons

All the buttons on this page are created with only HTML5 and CSS3. No Photoshop images or jQuery.

Effects like these can be cheesy all on one page, but my aim here is to demonstrate the different creative ways that buttons can be styled and made to behave. There is no reason to be boring on a webpage, within limits. Of course, buttons need to look like buttons or they are worse than useless and will drive site visitors away in a hurry.

The buttons above were all coded and modified by myself, though often starting from tutorials.

This is all pretty basic code anyway, and the point is not to show everything I can do, or to prove I am some innovator, but rather that I like to practice my coding and design.

I think the best way is the easiest one that does the job. CSS3 is trying to seize back some of the territory jQuery and Photoshop have carved out for themselves, and on this page, you can see it should do so eventually. There may not be much bragging rights or room to show off in CSS3, but clean, elegant standards fit many jobs.
