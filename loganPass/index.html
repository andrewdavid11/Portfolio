Logan Pass

Logan Pass is the high point of the famous and remarkable Going to the Sun Road in Glacier National Park. Beautiful hikes lead in several directions to remarkable views and are popular with hikers and wildlife alike. Goats, bears, sheep, and antelope frequent this area. Hidden Lake

At 3 miles round-trip, the Hidden Lake Overlook is one of the most popular trails in the park. Boardwalks lead to an overlook of the long blue lake and Bearhat Mountain, with animals often grazing nearby.

Mount Reynolds

Climbers visiting GNP typically journey up to the summit of Mount Reynolds, a sheer horn with excellent views, and multiple established routes to the top, home to many wild goats.

Learn more about safe climbing

Highline Trail

The Highline Trail is a long thruway that leads all the way North into Canada, closely hugging the continental divide and many mighty peaks with evenly spaced campgrounds for backpackers.
